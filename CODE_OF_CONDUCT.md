# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to making participation in the KONGALI SECURITY project a respectful, welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone.

We welcome contributions from people with different backgrounds, experience levels, technical skills, and perspectives.

Our community should be a place where people can collaborate constructively, exchange ideas, learn from one another, and improve the project together.

---

## Expected Behavior

All contributors, maintainers, users, researchers, and community members are expected to:

- Be respectful and professional.
- Communicate clearly and constructively.
- Welcome different perspectives and opinions.
- Give and receive constructive feedback.
- Focus on technical issues and project goals.
- Assume good intentions while addressing disagreements.
- Respect the privacy of other community members.
- Respect intellectual property and licensing requirements.
- Follow responsible security research practices.
- Report security vulnerabilities responsibly.
- Help maintain a safe and productive community.

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable within the KONGALI SECURITY community:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination.
- Personal attacks or insults.
- Threats of violence or harm.
- Hate speech or hateful conduct.
- Sexual harassment or unwanted sexual attention.
- Deliberate disruption of project activities.
- Publishing private or confidential information without permission.
- Sharing credentials, secrets, or sensitive information.
- Malicious impersonation.
- Trolling or intentionally hostile behavior.
- Repeatedly disrupting technical discussions.
- Deliberate misuse of security tools against unauthorized systems.
- Encouraging illegal or harmful activities.
- Any other conduct that violates applicable laws or community safety standards.

---

## Security and Responsible Research

KONGALI SECURITY is a cybersecurity project.

Contributors and users must ensure that security testing, research, scanning, monitoring, and analysis are performed only on systems they own or have explicit authorization to test.

The project must not be used as a justification for unauthorized access, disruption, data theft, credential theft, or other illegal activities.

Security vulnerabilities in KONGALI SECURITY itself should be reported according to the procedures described in:

[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md)

Please do not publicly disclose an unpatched vulnerability through GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, or public discussions.

---

## Constructive Technical Discussions

Technical disagreements are normal and can help improve the project.

When disagreements occur:

1. Focus on the technical issue.
2. Explain your reasoning clearly.
3. Provide evidence or examples when possible.
4. Avoid personal attacks.
5. Respect alternative approaches.
6. Be willing to reconsider your position.
7. Accept decisions made by maintainers when appropriate.

The goal of discussion should be to find the best solution for the project.

---

## Pull Requests and Code Reviews

Code reviews should focus on improving the quality, security, maintainability, and reliability of the project.

Reviewers should:

- Provide constructive feedback.
- Explain requested changes.
- Avoid personal criticism.
- Respect the contributor.
- Focus on the code and its impact.

Contributors should:

- Accept constructive criticism professionally.
- Respond to feedback respectfully.
- Update their work when appropriate.
- Ask questions when feedback is unclear.

---

## Reporting Unacceptable Behavior

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainers.

For security vulnerabilities, please follow the process described in:

[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md)

For general community conduct concerns, please contact the project maintainers through the appropriate private communication channel available through the official project repository.

Please provide as much relevant information as possible while respecting the privacy of everyone involved.

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for interpreting and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

Depending on the circumstances, maintainers may take appropriate action, including:

- Requesting that behavior stop.
- Issuing a warning.
- Removing inappropriate content.
- Temporarily restricting participation.
- Rejecting contributions that violate community standards.
- Removing access to project resources.
- Permanently banning participation in serious or repeated cases.
- Reporting illegal activity to the appropriate authorities when required.

Enforcement decisions will be made based on the available information and the severity of the situation.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces and activities, including:

- GitHub repositories.
- Issues.
- Pull Requests.
- Discussions.
- Code reviews.
- Documentation.
- Community communication channels.
- Project-related events.
- Other spaces where an individual is representing the KONGALI SECURITY project.

---

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers are expected to:

- Lead by example.
- Apply project rules consistently.
- Handle reports responsibly.
- Protect the privacy of individuals involved in reports.
- Avoid conflicts of interest when handling conduct issues.
- Take reasonable action to maintain a healthy community.

---

## Privacy

Information related to conduct reports should be handled with appropriate confidentiality.

Personal information should not be publicly disclosed unless required by law or explicitly authorized by the affected individual.

---

## No Retaliation

Retaliation against anyone who reports misconduct in good faith is not acceptable.

This includes retaliation against:

- Reporters
- Witnesses
- Contributors
- Researchers
- Maintainers
- Community members

Reports made in good faith should be treated seriously and respectfully.

---

## False Reports

Knowingly submitting false reports, fabricating evidence, or intentionally abusing the reporting process is not acceptable.

However, an honest report made in good faith should not result in retaliation simply because the reported behavior could not be confirmed.
